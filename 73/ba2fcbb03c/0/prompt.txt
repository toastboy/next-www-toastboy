<result>The question being asked is "where should we store points?" but the actual problem is "we have a fact about a game stored in the wrong place."

A game result is a property of the game, not the player. The outcome of a match — win, draw, or loss from team A's perspective — is a single atomic fact that belongs on `GameDay`. Points are then purely derived: given team A's result and which team a player was on, you compute their points with a two-line function. No storage needed.

The schema you actually want is a `result` field on `GameDay` — something like `GameDay.result: GameResult?` where `GameResult` is an enum `{ A_WIN, DRAW, B_WIN }`. Then `Outcome.points` is dropped entirely and replaced by a computed property.

But here is where I want to challenge the premise more deeply: do you even need `Outcome.points` or a `GameDay.result`? The `bibs` field already tells you which team wore bibs. If you also store goals, you derive everything. If you do not store goals, then a `GameDay.result` enum is the minimal sufficient representation.

The `PlayerRecord` denormalisation is a separate concern. Precomputed stats tables are legitimate for performance. But they should be derived from `GameDay.result` plus `Outcome.team`, not from stored `Outcome.points`. The derivation logic moves to one place — the service that builds `PlayerRecord` — rather than being scattered across every insertion point.

The real question you should be asking: what is the minimal set of facts that need to be stored, versus derived? Store facts once, at the correct level of granularity. Derive everything else.</result>
